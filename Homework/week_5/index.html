South America

Prevelance of Obesity

The Story

In many countries clean and fresh water is still not avaliable to all. At the same time, obesity is spreading world wide as an epidemic. While 10 % of the children in Peru do not have access to clean water, almost the same number of children (aged 5 to 19) are overweight. As both these issues concern (mal)nutrition, I thought it would be fun to visualize these extremes. On the left you can see South America, defined by the accessablity of AT LEAST basic water drinking facilities. If you click on a country, a barchart will appear that shows the obesity rates of all the children in South America. Hovering over the map, the bar of that country will light up. Wait, there is more! You can also pick for what gender (or both) you would like to see the obesity rates. Note that all these data concern data gathered in 2015.
